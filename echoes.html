Echoes in the Dark

Shadows coil where warmth once lay,

Your name erodes, a ghost in clay.

Every vow, a hollow sound,

Every breath—a burial ground.


You tore the spine from every page,

Left me drowning in the rage.

All the dreams turned into dust,

All the trust consumed by rust.


Tell me—was it love or just control?

A bleeding wound, a dying soul?


I hear you in the echoes of the night,

A ghost that lingers, feeding on my blight.

Your shadow’s stitched into my veins,

A silent curse that still remains.


The ring you wore now cracks like stone,

Cold reflections cut me to the bone.

Was I the pawn inside your game?

Or just a ghost without a name?


Every promise, barbed and tight,

A thorn wrapped in the blackest night.

You let me burn without a sound,

Buried deep beneath the ground.


Tell me—was I ever more than prey?

Or just a relic cast away?


I hear you in the echoes of the night,

A ghost that lingers, feeding on my blight.

Your shadow’s stitched into my veins,

A silent curse that still remains.


I tried to stitch the wound but it won’t close,

The past still bleeds like an open rose.

You walked away, unscathed, untouched,

While I was left to rot, undone.


Whisper my name with poisoned lips,

A requiem of your apocalypse.

I’ll carve your ghost into the sky,

And let you watch me say goodbye.
