Echoes in the Dark

Shadows crawl where you once stood,

Your name still lingers in the wood,

Every whisper is a ghost of you,

Every step feels like déjà vu.


You left me staring at the sky,

Searching for the reason why,

All the words that turned to dust,

All the dreams that bled to rust.


Tell me—was it all just smoke and mirrors?

A hollow vow, a fading picture?


I hear you in the echoes of the night,

A ghost that I can’t bury out of sight,

Your shadow’s in the static, in my veins,

Haunted by the silence, lost in pain.


The ring you wore now turns to stone,

Cold reflections cut me to the bone,

Was I the fool who played your game?

Or just another face with no name?


Every promise twisted tight,

A thorn wrapped in the blackest light,

You let me drown without a sound,

Buried alive, six feet down.


Tell me—was it ever meant to be?

Or was I just a casualty?


I tried to stitch the wound but it won’t close,

The past still bleeds like an open rose,

You walked away without a scar,

But I’m still chained to who you are.


Whisper my name like you once did,

Before love faded, before you hid,

I’ll carve your ghost into the sky,

And let you watch me say goodbye.
