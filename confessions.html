To the Woman I Once Called Mine,

Joyce,

This letter isn’t about punishment. It’s about truth, and what’s owed between souls when love has been mishandled...

So let me be clear: I release you.

You no longer owe me anything—not emotionally, not materially, not physically...

But I’m not letting this debt vanish into thin air. What you were given wasn’t charity. It was sacred...

If you still have any honor left in you, then do something meaningful with what I gave...

Build something. A space. A life. A relationship...

I no longer call you mine. But I do call you responsible—for where my love goes from here.

Aiden Taylor

My Dearest Aiden,

I am the woman who once held your hand in dreams...

I am deeply sorry...

I withheld truth when you needed clarity...

I am sorry for the ring… and for the petition...

And yet, despite it all… you forgave me...

And I will...

If another man enters my life...

I will offer coffee, a meal, a place to rest...

You taught me how deep a man could love. Now, I will carry that love forward. Not in bitterness, but in beauty.

Joyce
